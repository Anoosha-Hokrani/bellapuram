10 key solutions delivered

Each solution pairs a real-world urban challenge with a core algorithmic approach—mixing graph theory, greedy heuristics, dynamic programming, and streaming analytics to deliver measurable impact.

Smart Waste Collection & Recycling Management ⭐

  • Problem: Minimize truck distance while hitting time windows and recycling hubs.
  • Approach: Vehicle Routing Problem (VRP) with time windows; heuristics + local search.
  • Algorithms: Dijkstra/A* for shortest paths, Clarke–Wright savings, 2-opt/3-opt.
  • Outcome: Lower fuel use, higher recycling throughput, fewer missed pickups.

Smart Parking System for Commercial & Entertainment Zone ⭐

  • Problem: Real-time spot allocation and pricing to reduce circling and congestion.
  • Approach: Bipartite matching for spot assignment; dynamic pricing via demand curves.
  • Algorithms: Hungarian/Max-flow matching, sliding window analytics, ARIMA/EMA signals.
  • Outcome: Faster parking, optimized occupancy, increased revenue fairness.

Renewable Energy Grid Management (Solar + Wind) ⭐

  • Problem: Balance generation variability with demand and storage constraints.
  • Approach: Forecasting + constrained optimization across batteries and feeders.
  • Algorithms: Linear programming, unit commitment, Kalman filters, MPC.
  • Outcome: Smoother supply, lower curtailment, improved grid stability.

Port Cargo Scheduling & Container Allocation ⭐

  • Problem: Assign berths, cranes, and yard slots to minimize turnaround time.
  • Approach: Multi-resource scheduling with precedence and capacity constraints.
  • Algorithms: Integer programming, greedy dispatch, tabu search.
  • Outcome: Higher throughput, fewer delays, better yard utilization.

Public Transport Fleet Optimization ⭐

  • Problem: Route planning and headway control amid fluctuating demand.
  • Approach: Network flow for capacity, real-time control with feedback loops.
  • Algorithms: A*, max-flow/min-cut, reinforcement learning for headways.
  • Outcome: Reduced bunching, better coverage, shorter passenger wait times.

Water Distribution System ⭐

  • Problem: Maintain pressure and quality while minimizing leakage and energy.
  • Approach: Hydraulic network modeling and pump scheduling.
  • Algorithms: Graph simulation, LP for pump timing, anomaly detection.
  • Outcome: Stable supply, lower losses, energy-efficient operations.

Emergency Response Dispatch System ⭐

  • Problem: Dispatch nearest available units with route constraints and triage.
  • Approach: Nearest-neighbor with priority queues; time-dependent routing.
  • Algorithms: Dijkstra with dynamic weights, KD-trees, priority scheduling.
  • Outcome: Faster response, smarter coverage, lives saved.

Smart Housing Allocation System (Residential Zone) ⭐

  • Problem: Fairly match applicants to units under preference and policy constraints.
  • Approach: Stable matching with quotas and priority groups.
  • Algorithms: Gale–Shapley, fair allocation heuristics, constraint solvers.
  • Outcome: Transparent assignments, higher satisfaction, policy compliance.

Smart Market & Commercial Zone Price Analyzer ⭐

  • Problem: Detect price anomalies, seasonal trends, and demand shocks.
  • Approach: Streaming analytics and anomaly detection over seller feeds.
  • Algorithms: Sliding window stats, z-score/ESD, STL decomposition.
  • Outcome: Timely insights, fair pricing, reduced volatility.

Industrial Logistics & Supply Chain Manager ⭐

  • Problem: Optimize multi-stop freight with constraints and dynamic lead times.
  • Approach: Multi-commodity flow + route consolidation with service levels.
  • Algorithms: Min-cost flow, LKH for TSP variants, greedy packing.
  • Outcome: Lower costs, reliable ETAs, resilient operations.

Tech stack & methodology

Stack: Python/Go/C++ microservices • Kafka streams • PostgreSQL • Redis • Docker • K8s • Edge IoT.

Method: Model flows as graphs; optimize with exact methods where possible, heuristics when needed; validate via A/B and simulation.

Impact: Target 20–35% efficiency gains, 10–25% emission reductions, and measurable QoS improvements across domains.
